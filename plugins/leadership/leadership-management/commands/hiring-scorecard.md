# Hiring Scorecard

Create comprehensive hiring scorecards with role definition, competencies, structured interview questions, and evaluation criteria.

## Usage

```
/hiring-scorecard [role title and context]
```

Provide details about:
1. What role are you hiring for?
2. What team/department will they join?
3. What are the key responsibilities?
4. What is your company stage/size?
5. Any specific requirements or nice-to-haves?

## Process

1. **Define the Role**
   - Mission of the role
   - Key outcomes expected
   - Responsibilities
   - Success metrics

2. **Identify Competencies**
   - Must-have skills
   - Nice-to-have skills
   - Cultural attributes
   - Leadership qualities (if applicable)

3. **Create Interview Questions**
   - Behavioral questions
   - Technical questions
   - Cultural fit questions
   - Scenario questions

4. **Build Evaluation Framework**
   - Rating scales
   - Red flags
   - Decision criteria

## Interview Questions

### Phone Screen Questions
| Question | What You're Assessing | Strong Answer Includes |
|----------|----------------------|----------------------|
| "Walk me through your background and what brings you to this role." | Narrative clarity, motivation | Clear career story, genuine interest |
| "What do you know about [Company] and why does this role interest you?" | Research, fit | Specific knowledge, aligned motivations |
| "What are you looking for in your next role?" | Expectations alignment | Matches what we offer |

## Debrief Template

### Interview Debrief Agenda
1. Individual scorecards shared (no anchoring)
2. Round-robin: Each interviewer shares rating + key evidence
3. Discuss any concerns or discrepancies
4. Align on recommendation
5. Identify next steps

### Questions for Debrief
- "Would you want this person on your team?"
- "What are you most excited about?"
- "What concerns you most?"
- "Can this person do the job from day one?"
- "Will this person raise the bar for the team?"
